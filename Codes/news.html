Featured Articles

  • Unity Technologies has introduced a new royalty-free licensing model aimed at empowering indie game developers. Under this model, studios generating less than $500,000 in annual revenue can now access Unity's tools without incurring additional fees. This initiative comes after the company faced significant backlash earlier this year over its proposed pricing changes, which many developers found unsustainable.

    The new model is part of Unity's broader effort to rebuild trust within the development community and attract new talent. By easing financial constraints, Unity hopes to foster innovation and support smaller studios in competing with industry giants. This shift aligns with the company's vision of democratizing game development for creators worldwide.

  • The Game Awards 2024 is set to debut a new “Accessibility Innovation” category to recognize groundbreaking efforts in making gaming more inclusive. This award aims to highlight games that push boundaries in accessibility, such as features for players with visual, auditory, or mobility impairments. By doing so, the event seeks to encourage studios to prioritize inclusivity in their design philosophies.

    This new category reflects the industry's growing commitment to ensuring gaming is accessible to everyone. With major studios and indie developers alike implementing adaptive technologies and customizable options, the Game Awards hopes to celebrate and incentivize these efforts. The announcement has been widely praised, signaling a significant step toward fostering a more diverse gaming community.
